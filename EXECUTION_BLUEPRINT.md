## 2. CONVERSION FUNNEL & REVENUE MODEL

### 2.1 Full Customer Journey (Trainee Acquisition)

**Stage 1: Awareness (Top-Funnel)**
- Entry point: Organic search ("film internship South Africa"), LinkedIn article, blog post
- Agent 02 & Agent 05 drive content discovery
- Target: 40,000+ monthly visitors by Month 12

**Stage 2: Consideration (Mid-Funnel)**
- Content consumed: 3–5 blog posts, CEO LinkedIn articles, case studies
- Cineterns portal discovered and browsed
- Email nurture campaign begins (Agent 02 suggests topics, humans send digests)
- **New deliverable**: Weekly email digest of opportunities + insights
- Target: 15% of visitors → email list (6,000 subscribers by Month 12)

**Stage 3: Conversion (Bottom-Funnel)**
- Application submission on Cineterns
- Intake call with Placement Manager
- Tier 1 placement (SETA-funded internship) confirmed
- **Success metric**: 100+ new trainees enrolled per quarter

**Stage 4: Retention (Post-Conversion)**
- Monthly check-ins with Placement Manager
- Tier advancement messaging (tier 2 after first production placement)
- Alumni network engagement (From Intern to HOD documentary, case studies)
- **Success metric**: 60% of trainees progress to tier 2 within 18 months

**Stage 5: Advocacy (Long-Term)**
- Alumni become SAFA ambassadors
- Production companies recommend SAFA to peers
- User-generated content (testimonials, LinkedIn)
- **Success metric**: 25% of new productions come from existing partner referral

### 2.2 Revenue Model — Three Streams

**Stream 1: Government Funding (Grants)**
- Source: MICT SETA, DAFF, NAMB, NFVF, CSI foundations
- Agent 01 (Funding Scout) targets 12+ applications/year
- Historical close rate: 25% (3 grants/year closed)
- Average grant size: R500K–R1M
- **Year 1 target**: R2.5M
- **Year 2 target**: R3.5M+ (increased applications + higher success)
- Risk: SETA levy policy changes, funder fatigue
- Mitigation: Diversify across 3 tiers of funders; document impact yearly

**Stream 2: Placement & B-BBEE Consulting Revenue**
- Trainees earn stipends from SETA (paid to trainees, not SAFA)
- Production companies pay SAFA placement fees: R2,000–R5,000 per trainee placed
- B-BBEE scorecard consulting: R10,000–R25,000 per production company audit
- Target: 100+ placements/year × R3,500 average fee = R350K
- B-BBEE consulting: 15+ audits/year × R15,000 average = R225K
- AI curriculum licensing: 2 external institutions × R250K = R500K
- **Year 1 target**: R1.2M (placements + consulting + IP licensing)
- **Year 2 target**: R1.8M+
- Risk: Production companies resist placement fees; competing free talent marketplaces
- Mitigation: Emphasize B-BBEE compliance + quality guarantee; lock partnerships into formal SLAs

**Stream 3: Commercial Training & IP**
- AI Masterclass enrollment: 200+ students/year × R8,000 = R1.6M
- Advanced workshops (external creatives): 100+ students/year × R3,000 = R300K
- GreenSet licensing: Shared revenue on sustainability training = R200K
- Online asynchronous training platform (future): Estimated R400K+
- **Year 1 target**: R800K (masterclass + workshops, Phase 1)
- **Year 2 target**: R2.5M+ (with platform monetization)
- Risk: Content piracy, low enrollment if positioning weak
- Mitigation: Proprietary curriculum (Claude/agentic prompts), strong branding from pillars 1-3

**Total Year 1 Revenue Target**: R4.5M (R2.5M funding + R1.2M placement + R800K training)
**Total Year 2 Target**: R7.8M (with growth and new streams)
**Sustainability Threshold**: R4M+ annual revenue (covers team + operations + profit)

### 6.3 Platform & Technical Risks — Explicit Failure Modes

**Risk: Cineterns platform downtime or data loss**
- **Mitigation**: Supabase automatic backups (daily), Vercel edge failover (99.99% uptime SLA), Cloudflare DDoS protection
- **Monitoring**: N.White Systems monthly uptime report; escalate if <99% in any month
- **Contingency**: Manual placement process (spreadsheet + email) takes 4 hours/week if platform down >24 hours
- **Kill switch**: If platform unavailable >3 consecutive days, revert to pre-Cineterns process; N.White Systems owes service credit (prorated)

**Risk: Agent prompts produce low-quality outputs (Agent 02 content, Agent 01 applications)**
- **Mitigation**: Human review gate on all Agent 01/02 outputs; 50% rewrite rate acceptable, 80%+ unacceptable
- **Monitoring**: Weekly content quality score (readability, accuracy, SEO); funding application success rate tracked
- **Contingency**: If rewrite rate >50%, pause Agent 02; hire freelance writer (R8K/month) to take over; reduce posting frequency 50%
- **Kill switch**: If 0/3 funding applications approved in Month 2, pause Agent 01; revert to manual applications (Finance Lead + 10 hrs/week)

**Risk: Email deliverability (emails going to spam)**
- **Mitigation**: Brevo/Mailchimp reputation monitoring; SPF/DKIM setup verified; list hygiene (remove bounces weekly)
- **Monitoring**: Open rate target 25%+, click rate 5%+; if <15% open, audit email content and sender reputation
- **Contingency**: Switch email provider (1-week migration) if deliverability <90%; re-segment list by engagement
- **Kill switch**: If open rate <10% for 2 consecutive weeks, pause weekly digest; analyze why (content, timing, subject lines)

**Risk: Cineterns data privacy breach or POPIA violation**
- **Mitigation**: Cloudflare WAF (Web Application Firewall), row-level security in Supabase, no PII in logs, annual penetration test
- **Monitoring**: N.White Systems security patches applied monthly; audit log reviewed quarterly
- **Contingency**: If data breach suspected, inform CEO within 4 hours; legal review; user notification within 48 hours
- **Liability**: N.White Systems carries E&O insurance (R5M+ coverage); contract includes 2-month security response SLA
